>>> UNIT: L1.1 What is a gene?
>>> COMPONENT: TEXT
CONCEPT: Genes and alleles
QUALIFIER: 
LIB_TYPE: 
TITLE: Reading
BODY:
A *gene* is a stretch of _DNA_ that codes for a product — usually a *protein*. Different versions of the same gene are called _alleles_.

Key terms:
- *gene* — a DNA sequence that codes for a product
- *allele* — one version of a gene
- *protein* — the molecule a gene's instructions build

>>> UNIT: L1.2 Transcription and translation
>>> COMPONENT: TEXT
CONCEPT: The central dogma
QUALIFIER: 
LIB_TYPE: Diagram
TITLE: Reading
BODY:
The *central dogma* describes how information flows: *DNA → RNA → protein*. _Transcription_ copies DNA into *mRNA*; _translation_ reads the mRNA to build a *protein*.

[[image: central_dogma.png | The central dogma: DNA is transcribed to mRNA, then translated to protein]]

See the [pathway diagram](https://example.edu/bio/central-dogma) for the full picture.
<<<

>>> UNIT: L2.1 Alleles and genotypes
>>> COMPONENT: TEXT
CONCEPT: Genotype and phenotype
QUALIFIER: 
LIB_TYPE: 
TITLE: Reading
BODY:
An organism's *genotype* is its allele combination (e.g. _Aa_); its *phenotype* is the observable trait. A _dominant_ allele's trait shows even in a heterozygote; a _recessive_ allele's trait shows only when homozygous.
<<<

>>> COMPONENT: VIDEO

>>> COMPONENT: PROBLEM
CONCEPT: Monohybrid ratios
QUALIFIER: 
LIB_TYPE: 
TITLE: 
PROBLEM_TYPE: numerical
QUESTION:
In a cross between two heterozygotes (Aa × Aa), what percent of offspring are expected to show the *recessive* phenotype?
<<<
ANSWER: 25
TOLERANCE: 0.5
EXPLANATION:
Aa × Aa → 1 AA : 2 Aa : 1 aa, so 1/4 = 25% are aa (recessive).
<<<
